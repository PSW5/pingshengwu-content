## Overview

《折疊合聲》以光兼具粒子與波動的雙重特性為靈感，將數據資料轉譯為視覺語言，並於空間中播散。作品以數學公式重新描述粒子動態，探索聲音、光線與空間的交互關係。全作由程式碼生成，以像素（pixel）為單位，透過智能合約固定雜湊值（hash），衍生出兩百多種視覺結果。動態影像解構了像素排列，將其拆解為粒子，賦予各自的運動與速度。在圓頂投影下，這些粒子被重新拉伸，建構出空間結構。

畫面中聚散的粒子，將視覺焦點引向最基礎的光點，使我們得以凝視影像構成的本源。這些閃爍的微粒，其運動軌跡、明滅變化，皆成為被觀察的對象，在影像與純粹光點的定義間游移。空間中迴盪的聲響——或是與光點同步的震動，或是與之錯落的節奏，抑或是隱藏在光影變幻中的低鳴——皆與之呼應。影像的流動性營造出感知空間，促使觀者從被動接收轉為主動解讀，重新理解光與聲音如何交織、承載訊息，並展現其稍縱即逝的樣貌。

在建築般的尺度下，作品將感官完整包覆，透過光線與聲音的層層推移，創造出從二維邏輯躍升至三維空間的幻覺。《折疊合聲》試圖重新詮釋視覺與聽覺的界限，讓觀者得以「看見」聲音的形態，「聆聽」視網膜上的殘影。

Folding Harmonies draws inspiration from light's dual nature as both particle and wave, translating data into visual language and dispersing it within the space. The work re-describes particle dynamics through mathematical formulae, exploring the interplay of sound, light, and space. Generated entirely through code, with the pixel as its unit, the piece utilises smart contracts to fix hash values, yielding over two hundred distinct visual outcomes. Dynamic visuals deconstruct the arrangement of pixels, breaking them down into particles and imbuing them with individual motion and velocity. Under the dome's projection, these particles are re-extended, constructing a spatial structure.

The converging and diverging particles in the visuals draw the focal point towards the most fundamental points of light, allowing us to contemplate the origin of the image's composition. These flickering particles, with their individual trajectories and fluctuations in brightness, become objects of observation, shifting between the definition of image and pure specks of light. The resounding sounds in the space—whether vibrations synchronised with the light points, rhythms staggered against them, or a low hum hidden within the shifting light and shadow—all resonate in response. The fluidity of the image creates a perceptual space, prompting the viewer to transition from passive reception to active interpretation, to re-comprehend how light and sound intertwine, carry information, and manifest their ephemeral forms.

On an architectural scale, the work envelops the senses completely. Through the layered progression of light and sound, it conjures an illusion of ascension from two-dimensional logic to three-dimensional space. Folding Harmonies attempts to redefine the boundaries between the visual and the auditory, enabling the viewer to "see" the shape of sound and "hear" the afterimages imprinted on the retina.

### Portfolio DB entry: 光的時間量子 "Folding Harmonies: Quantum of Time Inside Light"

《光的時間量子》是一部為了營造沉浸式空間體驗而創作的音視覺作品，展出於國立台灣美術館的U108特別展覽空間。本作品包括了一個直徑14米的地面投影和一個360度的環形投影空間，結合了空間音訊設計，為參與者營造了一種如同置身無邊際的空間當中。

藝術家借鑒物理學中的量子力學原理，來解釋宇宙時間的行為及其特徵。通過描繪時間的不確定性和非線性狀態，以及光的行為與變化，作品將粒子運動的特性與時間概念相結合，創造出與時間膨脹、壓縮和延展相對應的視覺元素。此外，空間音響設計的加入為聽覺角度提供了更多可能性，使觀眾能夠察覺到時間的細微變化，並通過視覺與聽覺的體驗來探索感官維度的拓展。

此作品關注時間的本質及其對人類感知的影響。藝術家創造了一個挑戰線性概念的多維空間，邀請觀眾思考時間如何影響他們對現實的理解。通過在空間中通過聲音和視覺的擴展，觀眾被引領至更加動態和感性的時間體驗，沉浸在類似樹枝般展開的感官音視頻體驗中，超越了線性時間框架的約束。

The principle of quantum mechanics in physics is used to explain the behavior and properties of time in the universe. In the process of creating my work, I attempt to depict the uncertainty of time by contrasting it with the nonlinear state of time, as expressed in images and sound. By utilizing the characteristics of particle movement and combining them with the concept of time, I create visual elements that correspond to the expansion, compression, and stretching of time. Additionally, I incorporate sound design within the space to allow the audience to perceive the subtle changes in time, exploring the extension of senses through both viewing and listening.

《光的時間量子》是一個為著沈浸式體驗空間所製作的AudioVisual作品，首次發表於於國立台灣美術館U108特殊展覽空間。作品以14米直徑的地面投影、360° 環狀投影空間構成，環狀投影空間加上空間音訊的設計，使參與者彷彿身至於無邊際的空間中。

藝術家運用物理學中的量子力學原理，闡釋宇宙中時間的行為和特性。以光的行為與變化，透過影像和聲音來描繪時間的不確定性，與非線性狀態形成對比。作品將粒子運動的特性與時間的概念相結合，以光作為世界萬物的能量，讓時間與空間作為一種柔軟的存在，創造出能夠對應時間擴張、壓縮和延伸的視覺元素。此外，空間音訊的設計，讓作品有更多聆聽角度的可能性，讓觀眾能夠感知時間的變化，探索通過視覺和聽覺來擴展感官的範疇。

在視覺元素上是由一件在2022年發表的 generative art 「折疊合聲」做主題式的延伸，由波形皺摺的樣態，藉由部署光影來反應圖像空間上的皺摺，讓不管是聲波、光波，可以擁有另一種被閱讀的可能性。作品以思考時間的本質，及其對人類感知的影響為切入點。通過創造一個不僅是線性概念而是多維度體驗的空間，藝術家挑戰觀眾思考時間如何影響他們對現實的理解。透過聲音和視覺在空間中延展，參與者被邀請以更具動態性和感性的方式來體驗時間，包覆試的音像，讓線性時間框架如樹枝般的展開。

"Folding Harmonies: Quantum of Time Inside Light" is an AudioVisual work created for immersive spatial experience, premiering at the U108 special exhibition space of the National Taiwan Museum of Fine Arts. The work consists of a 14-meter diameter ground projection and a 360° circular projection space, along with spatial audio design, creating an infinite space-like experience for participants.

The artist employs the principles of quantum mechanics from physics to elucidate the behavior and characteristics of time in the universe. By illustrating the behavior and changes of light, the work depicts the uncertainty of time, contrasting it with non-linear states. The piece integrates the characteristics of particle motion with the concept of time, using light as a representation of the energy in all things. This allows time and space to be portrayed as a malleable existence, creating visual elements that correspond to time's expansion, compression, and extension. Furthermore, the design of the spatial audio introduces more possibilities for varied listening angles, enabling the audience to perceive the subtle shifts in time and explore the expansion of sensory realms through both visual and auditory experiences.

In the domain of visual elements, this is a thematic extension derived from a generative art piece introduced in 2022. Utilizing the morphology of waveform-induced undulations, it employs strategic light and shadow deployments to articulate the spatial creases within the image. This approach affords both acoustic and photonic waves an alternative modality for interpretation. The work focuses on the nature of time and its impact on human perception. By creating a multi-dimensional space that challenges linear concepts, the artist invites the audience to contemplate how time affects their understanding of reality. By extending through sound and vision in space, the audience is invited to experience time in a more dynamic and sensory way, enveloped in a sensory audiovisual experience that unfolds like branches, expanding beyond the constraints of linear time frameworks.
